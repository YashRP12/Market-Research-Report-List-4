<p><p>Animal milk replacers can be categorized into two main types: whey-based and skim-based. Whey-based milk replacers are made from whey protein, which is a byproduct of cheese production. These types of replacers are generally easier for young animals to digest and often contain higher levels of protein. On the other hand, skim-based milk replacers are made from skim milk powder, which contains lower levels of fat compared to whole milk. Both types of milk replacers play a crucial role in providing essential nutrients to young animals for healthy growth and development.</p></p>
