<p><p>Multispectral airborne electro-optical pods capture images in multiple wavelengths of light, allowing for the identification of various objects based on their unique spectral signatures. These pods are commonly used for surveillance, reconnaissance, and target acquisition purposes. On the other hand, hyperspectral pods provide even more detailed information by capturing images across hundreds of narrow bands of the electromagnetic spectrum, enabling precise identification and classification of materials and substances. Both types of pods play a crucial role in military operations, environmental monitoring, and scientific research.</p></p>
