<p><p>Cross-linked polyethylene cables are categorized based on voltage levels - LV (Low Voltage) cables, MV (Medium Voltage) cables, HV (High Voltage) cables, and EHV (Extra High Voltage) cables. LV cables are typically used for residential and commercial applications, while MV cables are used for distribution networks. HV cables are used for transmission networks, and EHV cables are used for long-distance transmission of electricity at very high voltages. Each type of cable serves a specific purpose depending on the voltage requirements of the application.</p></p>
