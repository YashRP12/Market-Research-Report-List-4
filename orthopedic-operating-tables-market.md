<p><p>Orthopedic operating tables come in two main types: powered and non-powered. Powered tables are equipped with electric motors that allow for easy adjustment of height, tilt, and other settings, providing convenience and efficiency during surgeries. Non-powered tables, on the other hand, rely on manual adjustments by the surgical team. While non-powered tables may be more cost-effective, they require more physical effort and time to adjust. The choice between powered and non-powered tables typically depends on the specific needs and budget of the healthcare facility.</p></p>
