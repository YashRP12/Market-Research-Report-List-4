<p><ul><li>Solid State Lidar</li><li>Mechanical/Scanning Lidar</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Solid State LiDAR is a type of LiDAR technology that uses solid-state components such as photodetectors and lasers to emit and detect light pulses. It is known for its compact size, durability, and reliability. Mechanical/Scanning LiDAR, on the other hand, uses moving parts like mirrors to steer the laser beam and collect data. While more traditional, this type of LiDAR can offer higher resolution and longer range capabilities. Both types play a crucial role in various industrial applications such as autonomous vehicles, robotics, and infrastructure monitoring.</p></p>
