<p><p>Intermittent type blood component extractors are devices that separate blood components in batches, whereas continuous type blood component extractors are designed to continuously separate blood components without interruption. Both types of extractors are used in blood banks and hospitals for various medical procedures such as blood transfusions and plasma exchange. Intermittent type extractors are suitable for smaller volumes of blood, while continuous type extractors are efficient for larger volumes and longer procedures. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring the availability of essential blood components for medical treatment.</p></p>
