<p><p>Absorbable sutures are categorized based on different types of materials used in their composition. Catgut sutures are made from the submucosa of sheep or goat intestines. Polymer chemistry sutures, on the other hand, are made from synthetic polymers such as polylactic acid or polyglycolic acid. Collagen sutures are derived from bovine or equine collagen. Other markets for absorbable sutures include those made from materials like polydioxanone or polyglactin. Each type of absorbable suture offers specific benefits and is used in different surgical procedures based on their properties.</p></p>
